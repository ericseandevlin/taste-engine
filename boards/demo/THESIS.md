## The thesis

### Values
- Every channel treats the ground as a working surface rather than a decorative element, keeping it flat, unmodulated, and free of gradients or atmospheric effects.
- Type carries structural load. Whether the choice is grotesk, editorial serif, or monospaced, letterforms organize the hierarchy before any other visual device is introduced.
- Restraint in color is consistent across almost every channel. Palettes are either monochromatic, anchored to a single accent, or deliberately limited so that each color performs a specific function.
- Density is kept low. Whitespace is not padding but a primary compositional ingredient that gives each element room to read independently.
- Metadata and taxonomy are treated as visible content. Small numerals, category labels, hairline rules, and index structures appear as design elements rather than as footnotes.
- Contrast is achieved through tonal extremes, black against white or cream against deep charcoal, before chroma is ever introduced.

---

### Visual laws (do)
- Use flat, single-value grounds. Pick one surface color from the dominant palette (#ffffff, #000000, #ffffcc, #ffcccc, or #333333) and hold it across the full composition without blending or vignetting.
- Assign one accent per layout. When chroma appears, it arrives as a single hue at full or near-full saturation (#ff3300, #ff66cc) against an otherwise neutral field, so it reads as a signal rather than decoration.
- Let type set the grid. Column widths, row heights, and spatial intervals should derive from the type size and leading of the primary text block.
- Make taxonomy visible. Index numbers, category slugs, section labels, and date stamps should be set small, set consistently, and placed in predictable coordinate positions.
- Use hairline or single-pixel rules as the only ornamental device. Rules separate, not decorate.
- Scale wordmarks or primary display type to a size that commands the ground without needing a supporting graphic element.
- Treat muted chroma (#cccccc, warm off-whites) as a ground choice, not a highlight. Reserve pure whites and true blacks for contrast moments.
- Keep interactive and static states visually distinct through weight or color shifts alone, without shadows, glows, or gradient overlays.
- Proportion spacing in multiples of the base type size so vertical rhythm stays intact when content length varies.

---

### Forbidden moves (do not)
- Do not apply gradients, blurs, or tonal washes to the background field. The ground must read as a single resolved value.
- Do not introduce more than one accent hue in a single layout context. Two competing chromas cancel each other's signal function.
- Do not use drop shadows, diffuse glows, or inner shadows on any element. Depth is created through contrast and scale, not lighting simulation.
- Do not use decorative typefaces, script fonts, or display faces with high stroke contrast for body or metadata copy. Structural type choices only.
- Do not crowd the grid. If low density is not maintained at smaller viewport widths, remove content before compressing spacing.
- Do not use color to convey mood or atmosphere. Color in this system marks category, state, or hierarchy.
- Do not mix warm and cool ground colors within a single page template. Pick one tonal axis and keep it.
- Do not animate elements in ways that alter layout geometry, for example expanding cards, morphing shapes, or reflowing text on hover.
- Do not use stock photography or illustrative imagery as background treatment. When images appear, they are content objects with defined boundaries.

---

### Convergence
This board clusters around one consistent proposition: structure is the visual content, and everything else is subordinate to it. The recurring combination of flat grounds, limited palettes, visible metadata, and low density points toward a site that communicates through organization itself rather than through imagery or ornamentation. What that means in practice is that the design system must be rigorous enough that any page, at any content length, reads as part of the same logical whole without relying on decorative elements to signal coherence.
